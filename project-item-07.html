Title: De-duplication of data entries.

Company: Motifworks

The companies recieved datasets which had expenditures made by multiple companies on various items like technical equipments, furniture, stationary etc. The data was collection of entries made by mulitple individuals and thus the company named varied despite the entity being the same. There are diverse ways in which a company's name might be represented, such as full names, different abbreviated forms, or variations with or without 'Ltd.'This made aggrgation of such data diffcult.

I undertook a comprehensive approach leveraging fuzzy matching techniques on word embeddings to discern phonetic and abbreviation variants of company names.

To achieve this, I initially delved into different methods for determining word similarities, encompassing phonetic similarity and various distances of word embeddings. The synergy of these approaches, coupled with judicious thresholding, proved effective in reducing duplicate entries by an impressive 80 percent. This not only addressed the issue of discrepancies in aggregated data but also laid a foundation for more robust data analysis, particularly when dealing with substantial daily influxes of information.

To enhance the scalability and efficiency of the solution, I containerized the developed code and orchestrated its deployment across multiple workstations using Kubernetes. This strategic containerization not only streamlined the execution of the code but also facilitated the analysis of larger datasets through parallelization. This deployment architecture ensures that the solution is poised for handling future data volumes effectively.

In summary, my work involved a multifaceted approach to identify and mitigate duplicate entries in company data, employing advanced techniques for word similarity and embedding analysis. The subsequent containerization and deployment using Kubernetes underscored a commitment to scalability and efficiency, paving the way for seamless analysis of extensive datasets in the future.
